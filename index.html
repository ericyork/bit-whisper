First-Year Composition

Integrated multimodal place-based curriculum. Students explore both physical and virtual campus places write profiles, design brochures, and create a web-based ePortfolio.

Second-Year Composition

Critical multimodal media literacy curriculum. Students study and research multimodal arguments and produce written, visual, oral, and web-based arguments culminating in an ePortfolio.

Basic Writing

Students participated in a mainstreaming model of FYC with plenty of feedback and workshopping, and produced print portfolios of their writing.


Technical Communication

Students learn to use emergent media to produce communication for technical and professional audiences, engage in user experience testing, and practice visual rhetorics. Students select from this work to complete a professional website.

Business Communication

Students study the professional contexts of communication and interrogate business-oriented genres. Emphasis is placed on multimedia document production, information design, and user experience. Students use these projects to create a professional website.

Rhetorical Web Design

Students practice rhetorically-oriented web development using HTML5, CSS3, and JS. Students author code, animations, and videos. Course work culminates in a fully-functional web portfolio.

Teaching ePortfolios

Graduate teaching assistants taking their practicum learned how to both create and teach how to create web-based ePortfolios.

Introduction to Literature

Students study a variety of literary genres as cultural texts, including fiction, drama, and poetry. Students produce literary arguments and analysis, as well as creative pieces in various genres.

I've taught this course both face-to-face and as an online hybrid.

Creative Writing

Students pursue creative projects in genre-fiction and poetry, producing works that range from haiku collections, to epic poems, to novels.

I've taught creative writing both online and as advanced independent study.

Teaching Integrated Multimodal Composition

Student-centered, socially-constructed, process-based pedagogies of multiliteracies deployed in order to interrogate, deconstruct and re-construct discourses and identities.

Teaching Professional Communication

Problem-based approach to professional genres and audiences in order to study and participate in collaborative knowledge-making.

Administering 21st-century Programs

Technorhetorics employed at the programmatic level to solve "intractable problems" (Wardle, 20014) in program administration.

Methods and Areas of Interest

What Clay Spinnuzzi calls "integrated scope" requires inquiry at all levels of a complex communication process, including the "macro-, meso- and micro-levels" of communication. To achieve this, I employ mixed methods primarily consisting of user experience, ethnography, and analytics.
